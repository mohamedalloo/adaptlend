DSCR Loan Requirements in 2026

By the AdaptLend Team · Content contributor: Moh Alloo, licensed mortgage loan originator · Updated July 13, 2026

DSCR qualification is about the property and the file, not your paycheck — here is the actual checklist lenders run. No W-2s, no tax returns, no debt-to-income math. Instead, lenders check your credit score, your down payment and reserves, the property's rent-to-payment ratio, and a short stack of documents. Every number below is a typical industry range, not a promise — DSCR is a non-QM product, and requirements genuinely vary from lender to lender.

The requirements at a glance

  • Credit score: many programs start around 620–660; the best pricing typically opens at 720–740+. Lower scores usually mean lower maximum leverage.
  • Down payment: typically 20–25% on a purchase — that is, 75–80% maximum LTV. Cash-out refinances usually cap around 70–75% LTV.
  • DSCR ratio: 1.0–1.25 is the standard band. Sub-1.0 programs exist down to roughly 0.75 with more down and a higher rate — see how California investors work sub-1.0 deals.
  • Reserves: typically 3–6 months of PITIA in verifiable assets; jumbo-sized loans and portfolios often require 6–12 months.
  • Loan amounts: roughly $150K on the low end up to $3M+ with some lenders.
  • Property types: 1–8 units, condos, townhomes; some programs cover condotels and non-warrantable condos.
  • Entity vesting: closing in an LLC is allowed and common — these are business-purpose loans.
  • No personal income docs: no W-2s, no tax returns, no employment verification, no DTI calculation.
  • Appraisal with rent analysis: a standard appraisal plus the appraiser's market-rent opinion (Form 1007 rent schedule).

How lenders calculate the ratio

DSCR = monthly rent ÷ monthly PITIA (principal, interest, taxes, insurance, plus any HOA dues). Rent of $2,400 against a $2,000 all-in payment is a 1.20 — a deal most lenders like. Two details decide marginal files:

  • Which rent counts. Leased properties typically use the in-place lease (often the lower of lease or market rent); vacant properties use the appraiser's market-rent opinion on the 1007. Some programs use documented short-term-rental income.
  • Which payment counts. Some programs compute the ratio at the interest-only payment, which can carry a borderline deal over 1.0 — with the amortizing step-up coming later.

What DSCR lenders do NOT ask for

No W-2s. No tax returns. No employment verification. No debt-to-income ratio. That's the whole appeal — your personal income is not part of the file. But "no income docs" doesn't mean "no scrutiny": lenders still pull your personal credit, verify the assets behind your down payment and reserves (bank statements, seasoned funds), and require a full appraisal with a rent analysis. The requirements people forget

  • Prepayment penalties. A 3–5 year step-down penalty (e.g., 3-2-1) is standard on investment DSCR loans. Some states restrict or ban them — ask before you assume, and know that buying the penalty down costs a little in rate.
  • First-time-investor overlays. Some lenders require a housing history (12 months of rent or mortgage payments) or prior ownership experience. First rental? Program selection matters more.
  • Seasoning for cash-out. Most lenders want you on title for a period — commonly around 6 months — before a cash-out refinance uses the new appraised value.
  • Entity documents. Closing in an LLC means producing the operating agreement, articles, EIN letter, and a certificate of good standing. Have them ready — chasing them mid-escrow is the classic avoidable delay.
  • Insurance. Landlord policy with adequate coverage, plus state-specific lines where required — wind, flood, quake. Taxes and insurance vary enormously by state and can make or break the ratio; see DSCR loans by state.

Requirements vary more by lender than borrowers expect.

Frequently asked questions

What's the minimum credit score for a DSCR loan?

Many programs start around 620–660; best pricing typically opens at 720–740+. A lower score usually caps your leverage, so expect more down.

Can I get a DSCR loan with no rental history?

Usually yes — vacant properties qualify on the appraiser's market-rent opinion (Form 1007). Some lenders add first-time-investor overlays, so this affects which program fits, not whether one exists.

What if my ratio is below 1.0?

Sub-1.0 programs go down to roughly 0.75 with some lenders — more down, higher rate. A larger down payment can also lift the ratio itself by shrinking the payment.

Can I close in an LLC, and will the loan show on my personal credit?

LLC vesting is standard. Most DSCR loans aren't reported as personal tradelines, but lenders still pull your personal credit and typically require a personal guaranty.
