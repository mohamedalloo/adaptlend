How lenders actually do this math

  • Personal statements: deposits generally count in full — the assumption is business expenses already came out before you paid yourself.
  • Business statements: an expense factor approximates your costs. Many programs default to 50%; a CPA letter documenting a lower real expense rate (say 30%) raises your qualifying income accordingly.
  • What gets excluded: transfers between your own accounts, loan proceeds, one-off windfalls. Consistent, explainable deposits are what carry a file.
